> I am reading out camera frames with the usb4rt drivers. The camera has
> 320 x 256 pixels and 2 bytes of data per pixel. If i read out smaller
> regions of the image, i have no problems. But when i try to read out
> the complete image : 320 x 256 x 2 = 163 840 bytes, i get an error
> when i try to allocate (kmalloc) a buffer that big. The limit seems to
> be 131048 (man-page kmalloc).

In my diploma thesis only 72 KB data are received, thus i had not this
problem.

>
> Is there a workaround for this ?

Unfortunately not. Try to create two or more callback URBs and send them
in quick succession. In theory it has to run.

We plan to adapt USB4RT to the RealTime driver model (RTDM). Thus
userspace driver can be written,
but the databuffers and URBs have to be in kernelspace (the
hostcontroller needs DMA buffer).
The usbcore will be allocating all memory (also greater than 128K).

At the time a student gives attention to port the stack to RTDM. Thus we
can't interfere very much.
